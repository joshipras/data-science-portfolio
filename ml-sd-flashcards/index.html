What These Interviews Usually Reward

SQL is table stakes

Strong candidates move comfortably from raw event logs to business metrics, especially with time windows, cohorts, and marketplace joins.

Marketplace experiments are tricky

Two-sided systems create interference, delayed effects, and metric tradeoffs. Good answers mention switchbacks, guardrails, and downstream behavior shifts.

Product thinking beats textbook math

The best answers sound operational: what changed, where is the bottleneck, who is affected, and what decision would you make next.

OR is most useful as intuition

Queueing, matching, resource allocation, and feedback loops help you frame problems more sharply without needing proofs.
